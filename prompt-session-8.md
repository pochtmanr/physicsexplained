New physicists needed (check existing PHYSICISTS first after Session 7 added Minkowski; only add missing):
— §12.1 gauge-theory-origins: **Hermann Weyl (1885–1955).** German mathematician. 1918 attempted to unify gravity and electromagnetism via a "gauge" (literally length-rescaling) theory — Einstein famously rejected it ("the rod and clock problem"). 1929 retooled the same idea as a *phase* gauge transformation in the new quantum mechanics, and it WORKED — the U(1) gauge symmetry of QED is Weyl's. Also: Weyl spinors, Weyl semimetals (named for his 1929 fermion equation), foundational work in differential geometry and group theory, Princeton IAS colleague of Einstein. Wrote some of the most beautiful prose of any physicist; "Symmetry" (1952) is still in print. Died at 70 of a heart attack outside a Zurich post office on his birthday.
— §12.2 aharonov-bohm-effect: **Yakir Aharonov (1932– ).** Israeli theoretical physicist. With David Bohm, 1959, predicted that an electron passing one side or the other of a shielded solenoid acquires a phase shift proportional to the enclosed magnetic flux — even though the electron traverses only field-free regions. Confirmed experimentally by Tonomura 1986 with electron holography. Also: weak measurements (1988), two-state vector formalism, Aharonov-Casher effect. Co-author with Bohm on the 1959 paper that bears both their names and ranks among the top-cited papers in 20th-century physics. Currently Professor at Chapman University.
— §12.2 aharonov-bohm-effect: **David Bohm (1917–1992).** American-British theoretical physicist. Co-author with Aharonov of the 1959 effect. Also: pilot-wave interpretation of quantum mechanics (1952, "Bohmian mechanics" — a deterministic alternative to Copenhagen that reproduces all quantum predictions), plasma physics (Bohm diffusion), philosophy-of-physics work with J. Krishnamurti. Blacklisted from US academia during McCarthyism for refusing to testify against Oppenheimer's circle; relocated to São Paulo, then Haifa, then Bristol, settled at Birkbeck London 1961. Died in 1992. The Aharonov-Bohm paper is the result he is best known for in physics; the pilot-wave interpretation is what philosophers cite him for.
— §12.4 classical-limit-of-qed: **Richard Feynman (1918–1988).** American theoretical physicist. Co-developed QED 1948–1949 (with Julian Schwinger and Sin-Itiro Tomonaga; Nobel Prize 1965 shared three ways). Path-integral formulation of quantum mechanics (1948). Feynman diagrams (1949). Manhattan Project (1943–1945, Los Alamos). Caltech "Feynman Lectures on Physics" (1963). Investigated the Challenger disaster (1986) and demonstrated the O-ring failure on national television by dunking one in ice water. Died in 1988 of cancer. **The §12.4 voice anchor:** Feynman's "Lectures on Physics" is the book every undergraduate reads on EM, and his approach to the classical-limit-of-QED is to start from the photon and recover Maxwell, not the other way around.
