New physicists needed (check existing 97 PHYSICISTS first after Session 5 added 5; only add missing):
— §10.1 larmor-formula: **Joseph Larmor (1857–1942).** Irish mathematical physicist at Cambridge. 1897 derived the formula for radiation from an accelerating charge — power = (q² a²) / (6πε₀c³), the defining classical result for how moving charges radiate. Also: Larmor precession (the precession frequency of a magnetic moment in an external field, eω_L = qB/2m); anticipated Lorentz-Fitzgerald contraction in 1900 with a scalar-aether model that came painfully close to special relativity without committing the conceptual break. Elected Lucasian Professor of Mathematics 1903 (Newton's chair). Knighted 1909. Strong opponent of Einstein's relativity even after the evidence piled up — Larmor held onto the ether to the end.
— §10.3 antennas-and-radio: **Heinrich Hertz (1857–1894).** German physicist who in 1886–1888 experimentally confirmed Maxwell's 1862 prediction that electromagnetic waves propagate at the speed of light. Built the first spark-gap oscillator (transmitter) and loop-antenna detector (receiver) — the first antennas in the world. Measured wavelength by standing-wave patterns; confirmed reflection, refraction, and polarisation of radio waves. Died at 36 of Wegener's granulomatosis. The SI unit of frequency carries his name. **He should have been added earlier** — Session 4 and Session 5 both mentioned him in prose but never linked him. Wave 1 must finally add him to PHYSICISTS.
— §10.3 antennas-and-radio (optional): **Guglielmo Marconi (1874–1937).** Italian electrical engineer / inventor. 1895 first radio-wave transmission over a measurable distance (~2.4 km across a hill in Bologna). 1901 transatlantic wireless reception (Poldhu, Cornwall → St John's, Newfoundland, 3,500 km). Nobel Prize in Physics 1909 (shared with Karl Braun). Also invented the directional antenna. Decide at author time — Marconi leans engineering more than physics, but his transatlantic-wireless story is the canonical radio-antenna pedagogy moment. Include if §10.3 prose wants a narrative hook.
— §10.6 radiation-reaction (optional): **Max Abraham (1875–1922).** German physicist who in 1903 derived the first classical equation of motion for a radiating charge including self-force — the Abraham-Lorentz equation. Pre-relativistic ether theorist; famously rejected Einstein's 1905 SR. Died embittered at 47. The Abraham-Lorentz-Dirac equation (Dirac 1938 relativistic extension) is the full classical radiation-reaction story. Add Abraham only if §10.6 prose wants the full lineage credit.
